Empowering Psychosocial Well-Being at the Workplace (2024)

How communicating micro-breaks can contribute to structuring breaks and creating social awareness.

Categories: UX, behavioural design, well-being, work culture, office


Noteworthy achievement: Finished my graduation project with a 9 and I presented my product at the Vital Work event which was hosted by the Workplace Vitality Hub at the High Tech Campus in Eindhoven.


Situation

More and more working adults in the Netherlands experience burnout-related symptoms. It’s most problematic among young adults between 18 to 35 years old. According to TNO and CBS (2023), almost a quarter suffers from these complaints and it is expected to rise to a third of the young working population in 2027. The overarching theme of the project can be defined as the ‘well-being’ of employees.

Task

The objective of this project was to find the crux of employee well-being in the office environment and to find a product solution to the problem by ‘research through design’. My job was to fully structure and lead the project independently and deliver a well-balanced and researched concept to the client, Royal Ahrend, including a working prototype.

Action

Obstacles have been identified through literature research, interviewing the target group and experts, and observing workplaces. In the ideation phase, Quick-and-dirty tests were used to validate ideas and hypotheses. With all the information gathered, a concept was designed. This concept was then tested in three experiments. Multiple days and in different office environments, to research behaviour and experiences.

Result

Ultimately, communicating breaks via a standalone product was quickly understood by the target group. The product was experienced as fun and helped people in different kind of ways. For more introverted people it was experienced as a friendly invite, the work rhythm of colleagues became more coordinated and, most importantly, more breaks were taken.

Reflection

For future research, it would be interesting to see if this concept is sustainable in use. A lot of similar products have failed in the past because it wasn’t used anymore after a couple of weeks. For this project, these shortcomings were analysed. By focusing on the collective and making it accessible for newcomers hopefully, habit formation will arise.
